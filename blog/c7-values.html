Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

Posted on November 21, 2014

My Values:

Achievement is something I highly value in my life. I value achievement because the happiest I have ever been was during my greatest achievements. I also feel that I have not yet achieved what I am capable of achieving. This motivates me to work harder in life, to take bigger risks, and to believe in myself.

In general, I try to live up to these values:

Disagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree

Accomplishment - 5

Achievement - 4

Happiness - 3

Relationships - 4

Courage - 3

Stability - 3

Persistence - 5

Advice?

Motivation and persistence are things I'm sometimes asked for advice about. The first thing I tell people is you can do anything you want. It may take time, but if it's something you are truly passionate about, you will find a way to make it happen. The second thing that comes to mind when giving advice on motivation would be to set a goal. Goals have a way of making life more meaningful because it gives us purpose. How can you make your wishes come true if you never have a wish to begin with? Set a goal and aim for something you think to be difficult but achievable. The only way you can fail is to not try.

My values will play an important roll while at Dev Bootcamp. I believe persistence is what has got me this far in the first place. Telling myself I CAN do some thing is much more empowering than telling myself I suck at something. There have been many times I have been frustrated while learning to code. It will be important for me to be able to notice my frustration before it happens. This way I can take action before my brain goes into meltdown mode. I'm sure this will be a useful skill to have in life, let alone DBC. I have found that meditating has helped me develop this skill of awareness of myself and feelings. Practice in this area will help me be calm and confident under all circumstances.
